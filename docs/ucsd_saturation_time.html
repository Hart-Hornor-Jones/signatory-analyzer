UC San Diego — Signing Saturation over the signing sequence

Drag the slider (or press play) to watch each group's saturation — the share of its faculty who had signed — accumulate as the letter filled up. Click a bar to see the names behind it (signers and non-signers); in trajectory view, click a line to isolate it and see running counts. Signature order is the time proxy; the faculty denominator is fixed, only the signers accumulate.

Reading it. Snapshot bars show saturation as of the chosen signature #; trajectory lines show each group climbing across the sequence, with a marker at the current #. Click a bar (or “names” in the line-isolation chip) to open the roster panel: signers in bold, in signing order, then non-signers in gray, alphabetically. Click a trajectory line to isolate it; click it again or press “show all” to reset. Joint signers are counted once in their home department; school and STEM denominators are a full department census, and roster names come from the UCSD General Catalog (core faculty: ladder + teaching professors + emeriti). STEM = sciences, engineering, math, computing + Cognitive Science; the cross-appointed Data Science institute is shown only under “by department,” not in the grouped totals. Senate service divides a campus-wide census of 2,115 Senate-series faculty (ladder, teaching, in-residence and professor-of-clinical series; UC payroll 2024, with 2022 filling gaps) into those who appeared on a San Diego Division committee roster between 2021–22 and 2025–26 (555) and those who did not. Prior letters divides the same census by whether the person signed the 2021 “Replace the Proposed California Math Curriculum Framework” or “Open Letter on K-12 Mathematics” open letters (64 did). For these two splits, signers who cannot be name-matched to the payroll census — emeriti, non-Senate lecturers, students, post-2024 hires — are excluded from both groups (41 of 346 UCSD signatures). Panel rosters may differ from chart denominators by a few names where sources are snapshots taken at different times.
